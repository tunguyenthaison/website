UNDERGRADUATE THESIS

(May 31, 2015)

  • Topic: Linear control systems and the regularity of the minimum time functions

  • Advisor: Khai T. Nguyen

  • Abstract: Consider the linear control system x(t)=Ax(t)+Bu(t)x(t)=Ax(t)+Bu(t)x(t)=Ax(t)+Bu(t) with the target 000. The function T(x)T(x)T(x) is the minimum time needed to steer a point xxx to 000. It is well-known that under the Rank-Kalman condition, TTT is just continuous in the reachable set. Our main goal is to study the regularity of T(x)T(x)T(x). We prove that TTT is a.e. twice differentiable. Moreover, we also obtain an explicit formula for the set of singularities where the optimal control changes its sign.
